My Recipe: Fried Rice

Introduction

Fried rice is my go-to meal for a quick, delicious, and satisfying dinner. It's the ultimate way to transform leftover rice and whatever vegetables you have in the fridge into something truly special. I love it because it's incredibly versatile, ready in minutes, and always hits the spot. The key is using cold, day-old rice for the perfect texture. This recipe is my personal favorite version, perfected over many years of practice.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups of cooked white rice, preferably day-old and chilled
  • 2 ½ tablespoons of vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 ½ tablespoons of vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • ¾ cup of mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots, corn)
  • 1 ½ tablespoons of soy sauce
  • ½ teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 3 green onions, chopped (about ¼ cup)
  • ¼ teaspoon of garlic powder
  • ⅛ teaspoon of ground ginger

Instructions

  1. Heat 1½ tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat (about to 80°C).
  2. Add the beaten eggs and scramble them quickly until fully cooked. Remove the cooked eggs from the pan and set them aside.
  3. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the pan, then add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Add the cold rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the rice is heated through.
  5. Sprinkle the garlic powder and ground ginger over the rice and stir to combine.
  6. Pour in the soy sauce and sesame oil, stirring to coat the rice evenly.
  7. Return the scrambled eggs to the pan and add the chopped green onions.
  8. Toss everything together and cook for another minute until well combined and heated through.
  9. Serve immediately while hot.
