Chocolate Chip Cookies

Introduction

Cookies dated back to Persia in the 7th century AD, where they originated. Cookies were first introduced to America by the Dutch in New Amsterdam in the late 1620s. Chocolate chip cookies specifically, were invented by Ruth Wakefield in the 1930s in Massachusetts. Chopping up a bar of chocolate and adding it to her cookie mixture, created one of the most popular cookie flavors of all time.

I chose this dish because I obtained the recipe many years ago from a chef during a birthday party, and making this cookie unlocks a nostalgic memory for me. I also really enjoy chocolate chip cookies because of how comforting they are, with a glass of milk on a winter's day.

Ingredients

  • Butter ½ cup
  • Soft Brown Sugar ½ cup
  • Sugar ½ cup
  • All-Purpose Flour 2 cups
  • Baking powder 2 gm
  • Baking soda 2 gm
  • Chopped Dark Chocolate 1 ⅓ cups
  • 1 Egg
  • Sea salt 2 gm
  • Chopped Pecans or Walnut (Optional) ½ cup

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 185°C.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix flour, baking soda, salt, baking powder. After you're finished, set it aside.
  3. In another bowl, cream together butter and sugar until combined. Add in the egg and vanilla and beat until fluffy.
  4. Mix in the dry ingredients until combined.
  5. Add the chopped dark chocolate and nuts and mix well.
  6. Use your hands or a small cookie scoop to make balls and place them evenly spaced on your prepared baking sheets.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for approximately 10-12 minutes. Take them out when they are just BARELY starting to brown.
  8. Let them sit on the baking pan for 2 minutes before removing and placing them on the cooling rack.
