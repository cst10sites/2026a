The best chocolate lava cake!

Introduction

You will fall in love with this Chocolate Lava Cake recipe with its rich molten chocolate center! Ready in less than 30 minutes, this delicious dessert is must!. All you need are a handful of simple ingredients that are probably already lying around in your house, and you’ll have this rich, decadent and fudgy chocolate treat in no time.

The origins of the chocolate lava cake come from two chefs with differing stories. French chef Michel Bras is credited with creating the dessert in 1981, developing a precise method involving a frozen ganache center that melts during baking or, in other words chef Michel purposfully created a cake intended to have a gooey center. Meanwhile, Jean Georges Vongerichten claims to have accidentally invented a similar version in 1987 in New York, when he underbaked a chocolate cake and discovered its gooey center. While Bras introduced the technique-driven version in France, Vongerichten helped popularize the dessert in the United States, making it a global favorite.

Equipment

  • Ramekins
  • Baking Sheet
  • Double boiler
  • Electric or stand mixer
  • Mixing Bowls

Ingredients

  • ½ cup unsalted butter (113g)
  • 6 ounces bittersweet chocolate chopped (168g)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar (50g)
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 450°F (232°C). Butter and lightly flour 6 (4- to 5-ounce) ramekins and place them on a baking sheet.

2. Combine the butter and chocolate in a double boiler and melt over low heat, stirring until smooth. Or, place the butter and chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl and microwave on high, stirring every 30-seconds until melted. Whisk together until smooth.

3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the eggs, egg yolks, sugar, and salt. Beat on medium speed until thick and pale yellow. Fold the chocolate mixture and flour into the egg mixture until well combined. Divide the batter among the prepared ramekins.

4. Bake for 6 to 8 minutes or until the sides of the cakes are firm set but the center is jiggly. Let the cakes cool in the ramekins for 1 minute.

5. Place a small dessert plate over the top of each ramekin and carefully turn it over, inverting the cake onto the plate. Let stand for about 10 seconds, then remove the ramekin to unmold the cakes. Sift powdered sugar on top and garnish with berries, if desired. Serve immediately and enjoy!
